the CRC in mavlink is so annoying. they add a "crc extra" byte to the end of the calculation that isnt actually in the packet. its a seed based on the message ID.
in software (edp-core) i just appended it to the buffer before hashing.
in hardware i have to wait until the payload is done, then do one more cycle of crc_accumulate with the extra byte, THEN compare.
cost me 3 days to realize my hardware CRC was failing because i was doing the comparison one cycle too early.

sept 5
how big should the fifo be?
mavlink heartbeat is 9 bytes payload + 10 bytes header = 19 bytes.
sys_status is like 31 bytes.
if the cpu gets an interrupt and takes 100 cycles to respond, at 100MHz (10ns) thats 1us.
ethernet is 100Mbps = 12.5MB/s = 1 byte every 80ns.
in 1us we receive 12 bytes.
so a 64-byte fifo is PLENTY big enough to buffer the start of a packet while the cpu wakes up.
